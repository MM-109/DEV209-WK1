About Nairobi

Founded in 1899, Nairobi has grown from a railway settlement into a major center for business, education, and international organizations. It is often called “The Green City in the Sun” because of its parks and natural spaces.

Culture and Identity

Nairobi is a culturally diverse city made up of many ethnic groups, languages, and traditions. The city blends modern urban life with strong cultural roots that are reflected in its food, music, fashion, and art.

Nature and Landmarks

Nairobi is unique for having a wildlife reserve just outside the city. Nairobi National Park allows visitors to see animals such as giraffes and rhinos with the city skyline in the background. Other landmarks include Karura Forest, Uhuru Park, and the Nairobi National Museum.

Gikomba Market

Gikomba is the largest open-air market in Nairobi and one of the most vibrant trading spaces in the city. The market is known for secondhand clothing, handmade furniture, and locally produced art. Visitors can also find Maasai handmade shoes made from recycled tires and decorated with colorful beads.
